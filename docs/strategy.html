What’s honest here — and what isn’t the problem. Survivorship is corrected and immaterial. The universe carries 246 EUR names that delisted/collapsed 2018→now, yet it held 0 of them into death: momentum buys winners, and a dying name ranks last long before it goes — so this strategy structurally never owns the corpses. The headline is therefore not a survivorship artifact. Capacity isn’t the problem either — every name clears a ≥100k/day turnover floor, so a small account deploys without moving a price.

The real caveats: (1) Regime — 2024→ was an exceptional momentum tape (defence + AI: Rheinmetall, Siemens Energy, Palantir); even the held-out +104% test figure is regime-specific and will not repeat. (2) Concentration — top-10, so a few names drive the curve; one bad blow-up hurts disproportionately. (3) Coverage — every name is priced off its home exchange × FX (the L&S model), so liquid foreign names (Seagate, Aker BP) are included with real data — active and delisted (home status separates a real death from a mere Frankfurt withdrawal). Residual gap: countries outside the 18 mapped (China/HK, Japan, Australia) aren’t in yet. (4) Mechanics — daily closes, €1/order, slippage modeled not measured, and past performance is not future returns. The out-of-sample test is the guard against curve-fitting, not a promise.
