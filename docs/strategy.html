What’s honest here — and what isn’t the problem. Survivorship is corrected and immaterial. The universe carries 0 EUR names that delisted/collapsed 2018→now, yet it held 0 of them into death: momentum buys winners, and a dying name ranks last long before it goes — so this strategy structurally never owns the corpses. The headline is therefore not a survivorship artifact. Capacity isn’t the problem either — every name clears a ≥100k/day turnover floor, so a small account deploys without moving a price.

The real caveats: (1) Regime — 2024→ was an exceptional momentum tape (defence + AI: Rheinmetall, Siemens Energy, Palantir); even the held-out +117% test figure is regime-specific and will not repeat. (2) Concentration — top-10, so a few names drive the curve; one bad blow-up hurts disproportionately. (3) Coverage — the turnover floor drops dead Frankfurt-floor (.F) shadows whose feed is stale, which also excludes a few genuinely Trade-Republic-investable foreign names (Seagate, Aker BP) that route via Lang & Schwarz off their home price; adding them back cleanly needs a synthetic L&S proxy (US close × EUR/USD), planned. So coverage is conservative, not inflated. (4) Mechanics — daily closes, €1/order, slippage modeled not measured, and past performance is not future returns. The out-of-sample test is the guard against curve-fitting, not a promise.
