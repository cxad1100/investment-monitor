What’s honest here — and what isn’t the problem. Survivorship is corrected and immaterial. The universe carries 716 EUR names that delisted/collapsed 2018→now, yet it held 0 of them into death: momentum buys winners, and a dying name ranks last long before it goes — so this strategy structurally never owns the corpses. The headline is therefore not a survivorship artifact. Capacity isn’t the problem either — the picks are liquid global large-caps (Nvidia, Palantir, Seagate via Frankfurt), so a small account deploys without moving a price.

The real caveats: (1) Regime — 2023→ was an exceptional momentum tape; even the held-out +406% test figure is regime-specific and will not repeat. (2) Concentration — top-10, so a couple of explosive names drive the curve; one bad blow-up hurts disproportionately. (3) Global/FX — foreign cross-listings carry currency risk and price on their home exchange; the Frankfurt fill can lag. (4) Mechanics — daily closes, €1/order, slippage modeled not measured, and past performance is not future returns. The out-of-sample validation Sharpe is the guard against curve-fitting, not a promise.
