Read the result as relative, not absolute — but not because of survivorship. The universe is survivorship-corrected (delisted/collapsed names are carried and liquidated by the graveyard, below), and momentum barely feels it anyway: it buys winners, so it almost never holds a name into its death. The real reasons the headline is optimistic are regime (2023→ was an exceptional momentum tape) and concentration (a top-k that a few explosive names dominate). The universe is the liquid German-exchange set — every name clears a ≥100k/day turnover floor, which drops the dead Frankfurt-floor (.F) shadows whose price feed is stale/broken (near-zero turnover). Daily closes only — intraday execution and borrow costs (for any future short overlay) are ignored.

How it works

12-1 momentum (the core idea)

Momentum is the empirical tendency for assets that have performed well over the past ~12 months to keep outperforming over the next month, and for recent losers to keep lagging. The "12-1" convention skips the most recent month: trailing-month returns show short-term reversal rather than momentum, so including it would fight the signal. Score = price(t − 21d) / price(t − 252d) − 1, using only data available at the rebalance date.

Eligibility & selection

At each monthly rebalance, a name is eligible if its assumed half-spread is ≤ 30 bps and it has ≥ 273 trading days of history with a positive last price. The top-15 eligible names by 12-1 score are held equal-weight until the next rebalance — turnover is whatever set difference results from re-ranking, no forced full rotation.

Costs & execution

Entries and exits at each rebalance are charged €1/order plus the ticker's live half-spread (in bps of the position size); names held across a rebalance are not re-charged. The cost-sensitivity table re-prices the identical holdings schedule at 0×, 1× and 2× these frictions — selection never depends on the multiplier.

No look-ahead, by construction

Scores at a rebalance date use only price history up to and including that date; the resulting positions accrue returns strictly after it. Unit tests assert that truncating future data leaves past scores and past holdings unchanged.
